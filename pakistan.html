PAKISTAN

Cricket in Pakistan began before the country’s independence, during British colonial rule in India. After Pakistan was formed in 1947, cricket quickly became the most popular sport in the country. The Pakistan Cricket Board (PCB) was established in 1948 to manage the sport. Pakistan played its first official Test match against India in Delhi in 1952, just five years after independence. The team earned respect for its fighting spirit and raw talent. In the early years, players like Hanif Mohammad and Fazal Mahmood became national heroes. Pakistan registered its first Test victory against India in 1952 and continued to grow in strength. During the 1970s and 1980s, the team developed a reputation for producing world-class fast bowlers and brilliant batsmen. Players like Imran Khan, Javed Miandad, and Zaheer Abbas dominated world cricket. Under the charismatic leadership of Imran Khan, Pakistan won its first and only ODI World Cup in 1992. The victory was a turning point, inspiring generations of cricketers. In the 1990s and 2000s, Pakistan continued to produce legends like Wasim Akram, Waqar Younis, Inzamam-ul-Haq, and Shoaib Akhtar. However, the team also faced challenges, including inconsistency and controversies. In 2009, international cricket in Pakistan suffered a major setback after the Sri Lankan team was attacked in Lahore, leading to a long suspension of home matches. Despite this, Pakistan kept playing strong cricket in neutral venues, especially in the UAE. In 2017, Pakistan won the ICC Champions Trophy, defeating India in a historic final. That victory revived passion and belief in Pakistan cricket. The Pakistan Super League (PSL), launched in 2016, helped rebuild the sport and bring back international cricket to the country. Stars like Babar Azam, Shaheen Afridi, and Mohammad Rizwan have since emerged as global talents. Pakistan reached the finals of the T20 World Cup in 2022, showcasing their T20 strength. Gradually, international teams have returned to tour Pakistan, signaling a cricketing revival. Today, cricket is more than a game in Pakistan — it’s a unifying force, deeply woven into the nation’s identity and pride.
